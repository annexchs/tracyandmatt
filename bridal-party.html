The Bridal Party

Kelly Najarian — Maid of Honor

Kelly is the one and only sister of Tracy. From day one Tracy has been so proud to be Kelly’s “big sistow.” From countless hours playing Barbies, horses, and pool rescue to traveling the world together, these sisters have a very special bond. Tracy has been so lucky and honored to share her childhood with someone as special as Kelly. Kelly has such a genuine heart, one that lights up with children, good food, travel, and family. Kelly and Tracy have a language all their own, one that stems from a lifetime of silliness, honesty, and true love for one another. She is currently receiving her degree in speech pathology from North Eastern University in Boston, MA and dreams of working with children with speech and language impairments.

Mary Tew

Mary is Matt’s sister and is the energetic mother of the two flower girls, and the wife to a very handsome groomsman. She is an elementary art teacher in Charlotte, North Carolina. Mary has a true passion for the creative arts and loves to share this love with her beautiful daughters. It doesn’t take long after meeting Mary to know that she is an extremely loving, passionate, and funny person.

Lauren Hammang

Lauren and Tracy met freshman year in their very first occupational therapy class. Years later their friendship grew very strong after they went to Nicaragua together on a service trip. Lauren is an amazing pediatric occupational therapist at Texas Children’s hospital in Houston Texas. Lauren has an incredible patience about her and is truly the best listener. She goes above and beyond in showing how much she loves and cares for the people in her life. Lauren has such a knack for always making the best and most fun of any situation.

Michelle Pomar

Michelle is the sweetest most lovable person you will ever meet. She has a true knack for always being able to make people laugh with her quirky, lovely, and charming self. Michelle was one of Tracy’s very first friends in college, meeting as hall mates freshman year. They ended up graduating together as occupational therapists 5 and half years later. Michelle is a dedicated occupational therapist in Connecticut.

Leah Hitchcock

Leah is the type of girl that shines so bright in every room she is in. She is Tracy’s cousin, one that she had the ultimate pleasure of growing up with. Leah has the biggest heart. She is also one of the most hysterical people you will ever meet. Leah is a senior at UMass Amherst. She is a devoted rower and captain on the university’s crew team who is aspiring to work in law or politics. Leah is majoring in political science with a certificate in film.

Tess Hitchcock

Tess is a true teacher in every sense of the word. Tracy had so much fun growing up with her cousin Tess, who from a young age was always very passionate about art and helping others to learn. Tess has the sweetest soul. It is rare to find someone who has a truly patient and kind heart like Tess does. She has recently graduated from the Maine College of Art with a master’s degree in teaching and is the art teacher at a middle school in Maine.

Madalyn Hull

Madalyn and Tracy met for the first time in Tracy’s drive way in Maine when they were ten years old. Tracy and Madalyn were instant friend cousins. They had the opportunity to grow closer as adults when they moved in together when Tracy moved to Charleston. Madalyn is such an intelligent person, who has a true ability of being able to listen and give incredible advice. She was the first member of Tracy’s family to meet Matt and give her seal of approval the day she met him. Madalyn is currently taking classes and working at as a medical assistant with plans to apply to medical school.

Evelyn — Flower Girl

Evelyn is Mary and Nathaniel’s daughter. She is a very smart and sweet 5 year old who is in kindergarten. Evelyn is a budding artist and enjoys playing with her dolls, crafts, and younger sister Penelope. She is currently practicing the art of swimming at her weekly lessons. She is a huge Disney fan and knows everything about each Disney princess, especially Elsa.

Penelope — Flower Girl

Penelope is also the daughter of Mary and Nathaniel. She has a very spunky, silly personality. Penelope spends her free time sucking her fingers, snuggling, and keeping up with her big sister Evelyn. Penelope especially enjoys eating.

Brian Cruse — Best Man

Brian is my older brother and co-founder of Unique Constructors based out of Charleston, SC. Brian has always been the best brother and role model anyone could ask for. He has taught me so many lessons over the years and while he may not want to take the blame for this, I can say without a doubt that I wouldn’t be the person I am today without him. Even my decision to come to Charleston would not have happened without Brian. While riding down Calhoun St. during a visit in my teens, a college student nearly stepped out in front of Brian’s truck, prompting an exclamation of “Damn college kid!”. My immediate response of, “Woah, there’s a college here?!” set the gears in motion that would eventually lead to me meeting Tracy and finding the piece I hadn’t realized I was missing. I will be forever grateful to that absent minded college student and to Brian’s assertive driving.

Connor Brown

Connor is my first cousin and has been my partner in crime since we were both in diapers. Every summer when we were growing up, Connor and I attended summer camp together. My favorite part about our friendship was that even though we only saw each other a few times a year, every summer we would pick up right where we left off without missing a beat. That same quality holds true today. We may only have the opportunity to visit once or twice a year but it’s always some of the best times of the year when we’re together.

Forrest Albaugh

Forrest is the co-owner of Unique Constructors and a fellow former Pennsylvanian. I remember the first time I met Forrest was in he and Brian’s “economy” apartment. At the time Brian and Forrest were spending a significant amount of time hitting the gym and I remember being a bit intimidated by this big guy doing pull ups in the living room. During that trip Forrest went out of his way to talk with 12 year old me and make me feel like one of the guys. I learned at that time that this big guy had an even bigger heart. Anyone who knows Forrest can attest to the truth of that observation and to the warmth he brings to any gathering.

Jimmy Joy

Before college I considered myself a bit of a loner. Something of a one man wolf pack. Jimmy and I met freshman year of college after I heard metal music being played at a bordering-on-misdemeanor decibel level and thought to myself, “whoever that is, we need to be friends.” When I discovered that in addition to having the ability to cram a 3000W sound system in an 800 square foot dorm room, Jimmy also shared my passion for all things dorky and nerdy, I knew that he was one of my own. And my wolf pack.. it grew by one.

Nathaniel Tew

Nathaniel is my brother-in-law and father of the century to our two beautiful flower girls, Evelyn and Penelope. When I first met Nathaniel I was 11 or 12 and hadn’t yet fully owned my own love of all things dorky and nerdy. Nathaniel would play video games with me and talk to me about all of the interests I wasn’t able to talk about passionately with my peers. He always went out of his way to spend time with me and give me advice; often opening my mind to new areas and ideas I had yet to encounter. These early interactions speak to the core of Nathaniel’s character. He is a truly compassionate person who makes all around him feel welcome and supported. I don’t think I would have found who I am as quickly as I have without his support.

Perry Spyropolous

Perry and I met in a game design class my freshman year of school when we worked together on what was hands down the best game in the class. It was a couple semesters later when Perry TAed my Python class that our friendship came into its own. When I think about Perry the first thing that comes to mind is LAN parties and deep discussions of topics far and wide. To the uninitiated, a LAN party is when you and friends get your computers together and connect them via a Local Area Network to play games. We would often get together and game for hours on end. During these gatherings, Perry and I would have in depth conversations across wide ranging topics. These conversations were some of the most stimulating and thought provoking I’ve had and are among my warmest memories.

Ryan Eleuteri

Ryan is the Owner and President of Charleston Mix, which produces some of the most delicious bloody mary mix you can find. Outside of his entrepreneurial endeavors, Ryan is the life of the party. He is always going out of his way to make sure everyone feels welcome and is having fun. I remember I was at a Charleston Mix event with a buddy of mine who Ryan had only met once before. Despite having a million and one things to do for the event, Ryan not only remembered him, but was genuinely invested in how he had been; even asking follow up questions to their brief conversation months before. To me that perfectly summarizes who Ryan is; a man who is empathetic to, and invested in, everyone he meets.
